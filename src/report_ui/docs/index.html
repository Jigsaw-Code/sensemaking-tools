The Future of Oklahoma

Executive Summary

  • 819 Total Participants
  • 7 Topics Identified
  • 41 Opinions Identified

This report summarizes the results of public input from 819 participants. From the participant responses, 7 high level topics were identified, as well as 41 opinions. All participants were anonymous.

Oklahomans articulate a compelling vision for their future, rooted in shared compassion, robust economic opportunity, and accessible education. They see a state where communities actively support the vulnerable, fostering belonging and enabling widespread thriving. This aspiration is amplified by a desire for safe, family-friendly spaces and a strong emphasis on education as the bedrock for individual and state potential, including practical life skills and affordable higher education. Residents also prioritize a government focused on core needs like jobs, infrastructure, and healthcare, believing that tangible solutions and transparency are key to rebuilding trust and unlocking the state's full promise.

This collective aspiration is significantly shaped by a clear understanding of the structural barriers hindering progress. Oklahomans identify low wages, the high cost of living, and inadequate access to funding, transportation, and childcare as critical impediments to financial security and workforce participation. They advocate for a reformed assistance system that supports, rather than penalizes, upward mobility. Furthermore, a strong consensus emerges around treating addiction and mental illness as health crises, alongside a call for public safety built on trust and opportunity, including law enforcement accountability and investment in preventative measures. These interconnected needs represent a profound opportunity to reignite the American Dream by removing obstacles and fostering an environment where all Oklahomans can achieve their full potential.

Economic Opportunity

6 Opinions
523 Total quotes

Oklahomans see a clear path to reigniting the American Dream through expanded economic opportunity. A key insight is that structural barriers, not a lack of desire, prevent many from achieving financial security. Low wages and the high cost of living are the most significant hurdles. However, significant untapped potential exists. Participants highlight a strong entrepreneurial spirit, eager for better access to funding and support. Others highlight the role that reliable transportation and affordable childcare could play in unlocking a willing workforce, as well as the need for skills training and education to connect Oklahomans to future jobs. Finally, a critical opportunity lies in reforming the assistance system to avoid penalizing families for earning more and truly enable escape from poverty.
